# Archipelago — Player Manual

---

## Welcome

**Archipelago** is a bridge-building puzzle game set across five island worlds. Connect islands with bridges to unite each faction — but watch out, rival factions' bridges can't cross!

---

## How to Play

### Goal
Every puzzle is a grid of numbered, colored islands. Your job:

1. **Build bridges** between islands of the same color.
2. **Match the numbers** — each island's number tells you exactly how many bridges must connect to it.
3. **Keep factions connected** — all islands of one color must link to each other through a chain of bridges.
4. **No crossing** — bridges of different colors cannot cross each other.

When all islands are satisfied, the puzzle is solved!

### Building Bridges
Tap an island to select it. Its number glows and nearby islands of the same color highlight. Tap a highlighted neighbor to build a bridge.

- **First tap** → one bridge.
- **Second tap** → double bridge (counts as 2 connections).
- **Third tap** → remove the bridge.

Bridges can only be built in straight lines — horizontally or vertically. There must be no other island between the two you're connecting.

### Reading the Numbers
Each island displays a number from 1 to 8. This is how many bridge connections must attach to that island:

- A single bridge = 1 connection.
- A double bridge = 2 connections.

When an island has exactly the right number of connections, its number turns green. If it has too many, it gently wobbles to let you know.

## The Worlds

Your journey crosses five island worlds, each with 15 puzzles.

### 1. Coral Shores
*Tropical islands floating in crystal waters.*
Small grids, one or two factions. Learn the basics here — most bridges can be figured out just by counting.

### 2. Emerald Canopy
*Mossy islands nestled in a deep forest.*
Double bridges appear more often, and the numbers get trickier. Pay close attention to which island has room for one more bridge versus two.

### 3. Granite Peaks
*Rocky outcrops jutting from mountain mist.*
A third faction arrives. Now you must think about territory — your bridges may block another faction from connecting.

### 4. Molten Caldera
*Obsidian islands over rivers of lava.*
Four factions compete for space. Blocking matters more than ever. Plan your routes before you build.

### 5. Nimbus Heights
*Floating islands among the clouds and auroras.*
The biggest grids and the tightest constraints. Everything you've learned comes together.

## Stars

Each puzzle awards up to three stars based on how efficiently you solve it:

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ★★★ | Solved with minimum possible moves — perfect! |
| ★★ | Solved with some extra moves |
| ★ | Solved (any number of moves) |

Your best star rating for each level is saved. You can replay any level to improve your score.

---

## Hints

Stuck? Tap the **lightbulb** button (or press **H**). The game will highlight one bridge that *must* be part of the solution.

Hints are unlimited. Using a hint counts as one move toward your star rating.

---

## Undo & Reset

Made a mistake? No problem.

- **Undo** (↩ button or Ctrl+Z): steps back one move at a time. You can undo as far as the beginning.
- **Redo** (↪ button or Ctrl+Y): steps forward again if you undid too far.
- **Reset** (🔄 button or R): clears all bridges and starts the puzzle fresh. No penalty.

---

## World Map

From the world map, you can pick any unlocked level. Levels are laid out along a winding path — completed levels display your star rating, and the next unsolved level pulses invitingly.

**Unlocking worlds:** Complete at least 12 of 15 levels in a world to unlock the next one. You don't need three stars — just solve them.

---

## Settings

Access settings from the menu or the pause screen:

- **Sound effects:** toggle on/off.
- **Music:** toggle on/off.
- **Color blind mode:** adds distinct shapes to each faction (circle, diamond, hexagon, triangle) so colors aren't the only way to tell factions apart.

Settings are saved automatically.

---

## Tips for Tricky Puzzles

- **Start with forced bridges.** If an island shows "4" and only has two neighbors, both must be double bridges. Look for these easy wins first.
- **Watch the edges.** Islands in corners or along borders have fewer neighbors, which means their bridges are more constrained.
- **Think about connectivity.** If two same-color islands would be cut off from the rest of their faction without a bridge between them, that bridge is guaranteed.
- **Mind the crossings.** Before building a long bridge, check whether it would block a different faction from connecting. Sometimes you need to leave a corridor open.
- **Use undo freely.** There's no penalty. Try something, see what follows, and undo if it doesn't work out.
